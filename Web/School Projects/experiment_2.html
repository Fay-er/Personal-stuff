Working of Thermal Power Plants

Thermal Power Plants are a type of a plant in which electricity is produced from fossil fuels- mainly coal.


Large amount of fossil fuels are burnt every day in power stations to heat up water to produce steam which further turns the turbine to generate electricity.


The transmission of electricity is more efficient than transporting coal or petroleum over the same distance. Therefore, many thermal power plants are set up near coal or oil fields. The term thermal power plant is used since fuel is burnt to produce heat energy which is converted into electrical energy.
